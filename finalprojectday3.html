Lets explore three categories of birds in Ventura: Tree Birds, Water Birds and Birds of Prey


A.Tree birds


1. House Finches

  • Habitat:in human created buildings, lawns in urban areas. In rural areas tht are found in barns and stables.
  • Food;they eat mainly plant materials. In the wild, they eat seeds, buds and fruits.
  • Social Behavior: very social. In groups or flocks. You can see them on powerlines or the highest point of a tree.
  • Overview of Finches

2. Red Headed Acorn Woodpecker

  • Habitat:in oak and pine oak woodlands, more common in the mountains.
  • Food:acorns and insects. They drill holes in oak trees and store the seeds.
  • Social Behavior: very social. Cooperative reeding that leads to destruction of some chicks.
  • overview of Arcon Woodpecker

3. California Quail

  • Habitat:in sagebrush and chaparral and backyards with birdseed at ground level
  • Food;70% vegetarian diet; including berries, acorns and invertebrates
  • Social Behavior very social, living in large groups of more than 75. They are usually at the ground level and hide in brush and shrubs for protection.
  • Overview of California Quail

B.Water birds


1. Great Blue Heron

  • Habitat: in freshwater and saltwater areas. Breeding colonies are closer to water.
  • Food: will eat anything in striking distance; including fish, small reptiles, insects and small birds<./li>
  • Social Behavior: very social with elaborate courtship and pair-bonding displays. Breeding colonies can be up to several hundred pairs.
